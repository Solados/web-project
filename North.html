Sakaka
Sakaka is the agricultural heart of Al-Jouf, known for vast olive groves and the striking Rajajil standing stones. The area combines museums and archaeological ruins with lively markets and olive-oil traditions. Travelers come for rural landscapes, local cuisine, and a window into northern agricultural life.

Arar
Arar is a regional center celebrated for its Bedouin heritage and bustling markets. Natural highlights include expansive desert panoramas and Wadi Arar ‚Äî one of the largest wadis in the north, stretching roughly 190 kilometers. The city's frontier culture and lively bazaars offer an authentic northern Saudi experience.

Umluj
Umluj is a coastal gem on the Red Sea, prized for clear waters, coral reefs and scenic islands ideal for boat trips and snorkeling. Its fishing villages, sandy beaches, and island escapes give visitors a distinct seaside flavor compared with inland northern destinations. Travelers prize Umluj for marine adventures and tranquil island scenery.

Overview of the Northern Region

The Northern Region of Saudi Arabia is rich in history, rugged landscapes and coastal gems. From rock art and historic forts to olive valleys, long wadis and Red Sea islands, the north offers diverse experiences for cultural and nature travelers.
