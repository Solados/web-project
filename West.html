western of Saudi Arabia
Makkah
Makkah is a holy city in Saudi Arabia and the birthplace of Prophet Muhammad. It is the center of Islam and home to the Kaaba, where millions of Muslims visit each year for Hajj and Umrah. Pilgrims come from around the world; the city blends deep spiritual significance with vibrant markets, religious services, and hospitality for visiting worshippers, and modern infrastructure supporting pilgrimage logistics.
western of Saudi Arabia
Jeddah
Jeddah is a major city on the western coast of Saudi Arabia. It is known as the gateway to Makkah and is famous for its beautiful Red Sea coastline, modern buildings, and rich cultural history. Jeddah is also an important center for trade and tourism in the Kingdom. The city offers museums, diverse cuisine, lively art festivals, and waterfront promenades that attract visitors year-round.
western of Saudi Arabia
Taif
Taif is a city in western Saudi Arabia known for its cool weather, mountains, and famous Taif roses. It is a popular summer destination and an important center for agriculture and tourism. Visitors enjoy rose gardens, seasonal rose festivals, historic sites, and mountain resorts with traditional markets and local handicrafts to explore. The cooler climate makes it a refreshing escape during hotter months.

western of Saudi Arabia
Yanbu
Yanbu is a coastal city in western Saudi Arabia on the Red Sea. It is known for its beautiful beaches, modern industrial areas, and as an important port and commercial center. Visitors enjoy diving, beach resorts, seafood, and a growing tourism scene that complements its industry and port activities, offering both leisure and business amenities. Well-connected transport makes it easy to reach from larger cities.
